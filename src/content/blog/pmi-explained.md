PMI adds hundreds of dollars to your monthly payment — and most homeowners don't realize they can often remove it years before their lender does automatically. Here's everything you need to know.

## What is PMI?

Private Mortgage Insurance protects **the lender** — not you — if you default. It's required on conventional loans when your down payment is less than 20% of the purchase price.

PMI provides zero benefit to you. It doesn't protect your credit, your home, or your equity. It's purely a risk premium the lender charges for taking on a higher loan-to-value (LTV) loan.

## How much does PMI cost?

PMI typically costs 0.5–1.5% of your loan amount per year, paid monthly. Your exact rate depends on down payment percentage and credit score:

| Down Payment | Credit Score | Annual PMI Rate | Monthly (on $350K loan) |
|-------------|-------------|----------------|------------------------|
| 3–5% | 720+ | 0.85–1.0% | $248–$292 |
| 5–10% | 720+ | 0.65–0.85% | $190–$248 |
| 10–15% | 720+ | 0.45–0.65% | $131–$190 |
| 15–20% | 720+ | 0.25–0.45% | $73–$131 |
| 3–5% | 660–719 | 1.2–1.5% | $350–$438 |

Over the life of a loan before reaching 20% equity, PMI can cost $15,000–$50,000+ — a significant sum that goes entirely to the lender.

## How to avoid PMI entirely

**1. Put 20% down** — the simplest solution.

**2. Piggyback loan (80/10/10):** Take a first mortgage for 80%, a second loan (HELOC or home equity loan) for 10%, and put 10% down. No PMI required, though you have two loans with two payments.

**3. VA loan:** Zero down payment, zero PMI — if you're a qualifying veteran, active duty service member, or surviving spouse. The best mortgage product available if you qualify.

**4. Lender-paid PMI (LPMI):** The lender pays PMI upfront in exchange for a slightly higher interest rate (typically +0.25–0.5%). Your monthly payment may be lower, but the rate is permanent — you can't "remove" lender-paid PMI without refinancing.

**5. Single-premium PMI:** Pay PMI as a lump sum at closing instead of monthly. Makes sense if you have extra cash and plan to stay long-term.

## The law: when your lender MUST remove PMI

The **Homeowners Protection Act (HPA)** mandates:

- **Automatic cancellation:** PMI must be cancelled when your loan balance reaches **78% of the original purchase price**, based on your scheduled payments — as long as you're current.

- **Requested cancellation:** You have the right to request PMI removal when you reach **80% LTV** based on original value. You must be current on payments with a good payment history.

- **Maximum duration:** PMI must end at the halfway point of your loan term (year 15 of a 30-year loan) — regardless of LTV.

<div class="warning"><strong>Important:</strong> These rules apply to conventional loans. FHA MIP works differently — if your down payment was under 10%, MIP typically lasts the entire loan term.</div>

## How to remove PMI early (step by step)

### Method 1: Request based on original value
1. Track your loan balance until it reaches 80% of the original purchase price
2. Call your loan servicer and request PMI cancellation in writing
3. Confirm your payment history is current with no 30-day late payments in the past 12 months
4. PMI must be removed within 30 days of meeting requirements

### Method 2: Appraisal-based removal (appreciate your way out)
1. Estimate your current home value using Zillow, Redfin, or a local agent's CMA
2. If value has risen enough to put you at 80% LTV on current value, request a formal appraisal
3. Pay appraisal cost ($400–700) — worth it if you'll save hundreds monthly
4. Submit appraisal to servicer with written PMI cancellation request
5. Most servicers require 2+ years of on-time payments before allowing appraisal-based removal

### Method 3: Refinance
If rates have dropped or your home has significantly appreciated, refinancing to a new conventional loan with 20%+ equity eliminates PMI entirely — though you'll pay closing costs.

## FHA loans: MIP is different

FHA Mortgage Insurance Premium (MIP) is NOT private mortgage insurance — it's a government fee that works differently:

- **Upfront MIP:** 1.75% of loan amount paid at closing (can be rolled into loan)
- **Annual MIP:** 0.5–0.85% of loan amount, paid monthly
- **Duration:** For loans with <10% down, MIP lasts the **entire loan term** — it cannot be removed without refinancing

If you have an FHA loan with less than 10% down and your credit has improved, refinancing to a conventional loan once you reach 20% equity eliminates all mortgage insurance and can save $200–400/month.
