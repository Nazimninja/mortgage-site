Paying off your mortgage early is one of the highest guaranteed returns available to most Americans. Here are six strategies with real numbers — so you can choose the one that fits your budget.

## Why extra payments matter so much

On a $400,000 mortgage at 7% for 30 years, you'll pay **$558,036 in total interest** — almost 1.4× the original loan amount. Extra payments attack this directly by reducing the principal that generates future interest.

The math is asymmetric: an extra $200 paid in month 1 saves far more than $200 paid in year 25, because it eliminates compounding interest across the entire remaining term.

## Strategy 1: Fixed extra monthly payment

The simplest approach. Add a fixed amount to every payment, earmarked for principal only.

| Extra Monthly | Interest Saved | Years Saved |
|--------------|---------------|------------|
| $100/month | $44,000 | 2.9 years |
| $200/month | $82,000 | 5.7 years |
| $500/month | $165,000 | 11.2 years |
| $1,000/month | $238,000 | 17.1 years |

*Based on $400,000 at 7%, 30-year term*

<div class="tip"><strong>Pro tip:</strong> Most lenders allow extra principal payments with no penalty. Write "apply to principal" on your check, or select "extra principal payment" when paying online to ensure it doesn't just apply to next month's payment.</div>

## Strategy 2: Biweekly payments

Instead of 12 monthly payments, you make half your payment every 2 weeks. Result: 26 half-payments = 13 full payments per year — one extra payment annually, with zero impact on monthly cash flow since you pay aligned to your paycheck schedule.

**On $400,000 at 7%:**
- Interest saved: ~$45,000
- Time saved: ~4 years
- Extra annual cost: just one half-payment (~$1,330)

Most lenders offer a biweekly payment program. If yours doesn't, simply make one extra full payment per year manually — same result.

## Strategy 3: Annual lump sum payments

Apply windfalls — tax refunds, bonuses, inheritances — directly to principal once a year.

| Annual Lump Sum | Interest Saved | Years Saved |
|----------------|---------------|------------|
| $1,000/year | $20,000 | 1.8 years |
| $3,000/year | $58,000 | 4.5 years |
| $5,000/year | $90,000 | 6.8 years |
| $10,000/year | $154,000 | 10.9 years |

The average US tax refund is ~$3,000 — enough to save $58,000 in interest and nearly 5 years if applied consistently.

## Strategy 4: Refinance to a shorter term

Refinancing from a 30-year to a 15-year loan typically reduces your rate by 0.5–0.75% AND cuts your term in half — dramatically reducing total interest.

**Example: $350,000 remaining balance**

| Scenario | Monthly P&I | Total Interest | Paid Off |
|----------|------------|---------------|---------|
| Keep 30yr at 7.0% | $2,329 | $488,440 total | 2053 |
| Refi to 15yr at 6.25% | $3,002 | $190,360 total | 2038 |
| **Savings** | **-$673/mo** | **+$298,080 saved** | **15 years earlier** |

The tradeoff: $673 more per month. Only refinance to a shorter term if you can genuinely afford the higher payment without financial stress.

## Strategy 5: Round up your payment

Round your payment to the nearest $50 or $100. If your payment is $2,661, pay $2,700.

This requires zero budgeting effort and saves thousands:
- $2,661 → $2,700 (+$39/mo): saves ~$19,000, pays off 1.5 years early
- $2,661 → $2,750 (+$89/mo): saves ~$40,000, pays off 3.3 years early
- $2,661 → $2,800 (+$139/mo): saves ~$60,000, pays off 4.9 years early

## Strategy 6: Mortgage recasting

If you receive a large windfall ($25,000+), you can make a lump-sum principal payment and ask your lender to **recast** the loan. The lender recalculates your monthly payment based on the new lower balance, keeping your existing rate and remaining term.

Recasting fees: $150–500 (far cheaper than refinancing's $3,000–10,000).

**Example:** $350,000 balance, 25 years remaining at 7%. You pay $50,000 lump sum. Lender recasts: new balance $300,000, same rate, same term → monthly payment drops from $2,475 to $2,121 (-$354/month).

## Should you pay off your mortgage early?

Consider these factors first:

1. **Emergency fund:** Always maintain 3–6 months expenses. Don't deplete it for extra payments.
2. **High-interest debt:** Pay off credit cards (20%+) before extra mortgage payments (7%).
3. **401k match:** Always capture the full employer match before extra mortgage payments — it's free money.
4. **Mortgage rate vs investment returns:** At 7%, your mortgage is a guaranteed 7% return. Long-term stock market averages ~10%. The math slightly favors investing over paying the mortgage early — but paying off debt is risk-free and emotionally valuable.
