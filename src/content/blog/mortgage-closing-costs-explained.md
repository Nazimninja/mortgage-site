Closing costs blindside thousands of buyers every year. You budget for the down payment, then discover you need another $8,000–$20,000 at the closing table. Here is every fee explained — and how to reduce them.

## How much are closing costs?

Closing costs typically run **2–5% of the loan amount**. On a $400,000 loan, that's $8,000–$20,000 paid at closing — on top of your down payment.

| Loan Amount | Low (2%) | Average (3.5%) | High (5%) |
|-------------|---------|---------------|---------|
| $250,000 | $5,000 | $8,750 | $12,500 |
| $400,000 | $8,000 | $14,000 | $20,000 |
| $600,000 | $12,000 | $21,000 | $30,000 |

## Every closing cost broken down

### Lender fees
- **Origination fee:** 0.5–1% of loan. Covers lender's processing costs. Negotiable.
- **Application fee:** $0–500. Many lenders have eliminated this.
- **Underwriting fee:** $400–900. Covers cost of reviewing your file.
- **Rate lock fee:** Sometimes charged; often waived.
- **Discount points:** Optional — 1% per point to buy down your rate.

### Third-party fees
- **Appraisal:** $400–700. Required by lender to confirm home value.
- **Home inspection:** $300–600. Technically optional but always recommended.
- **Title search:** $150–400. Searches public records for liens or ownership disputes.
- **Title insurance (lender's policy):** $500–$1,500. Protects lender against title defects. Required.
- **Title insurance (owner's policy):** $500–$1,000. Protects you. Optional but strongly recommended.
- **Settlement/closing fee:** $500–$1,500. Paid to title company or attorney managing closing.
- **Survey:** $400–700. May be required in some states or rural properties.

### Government fees
- **Recording fees:** $25–250. County charges to record deed transfer.
- **Transfer taxes:** Varies wildly by state — 0% (many states) to 2%+ (NYC, PA, DE).

### Prepaid items (not really "costs" — you're pre-funding accounts)
- **Homeowner's insurance:** First year premium paid at closing ($1,000–$2,500).
- **Prepaid interest:** Interest from closing date to end of month ($0–$2,000).
- **Property tax escrow:** 2–6 months of property taxes collected upfront ($1,000–$8,000 depending on area and timing).
- **Insurance escrow:** 2–3 months of insurance premium.

## The Loan Estimate: your rights

Within 3 business days of application, your lender must provide a **Loan Estimate** — a standardized 3-page document showing all estimated costs. Three days before closing, you receive the **Closing Disclosure** with final numbers.

Compare these carefully. Certain fees cannot increase at all (origination charges), some can increase up to 10% (third-party fees your lender selects), and others can change without limit (prepaid taxes).

## Proven ways to reduce closing costs

**Negotiate lender fees:** Origination fees, underwriting fees, and application fees are negotiable. Ask directly: "Can you reduce or waive the origination fee?"

**Shop for title insurance:** In most states, you can choose your title company. Get 2–3 quotes — prices vary significantly.

**Request lender credits:** Accept a slightly higher interest rate (0.125–0.25%) in exchange for lender credits that offset closing costs. Good if you plan to refinance or sell within 5 years.

**Ask seller to pay costs:** In a buyer's market, ask the seller to contribute 2–3% toward closing costs. Conventional loans allow seller concessions up to 3–9% depending on down payment.

**Close at end of month:** Prepaid interest = days from closing to month end × daily interest. Closing on the 28th instead of the 1st saves ~27 days of prepaid interest.

**Roll costs into loan:** Most loans allow you to finance closing costs into the loan balance. You pay slightly more interest but preserve cash. Only do this if you plan to stay long-term.

**No-closing-cost loans:** Some lenders advertise zero closing costs — they're rolled into a higher rate. Compare the total cost over your expected time in the home.
